Understanding Geopolitics in the 21st Century

Geopolitics in the 21st century is a dynamic field shaped by global competition, technological innovation, and new forms of power projection. Unlike the bipolar order of the Cold War, the world today faces multipolar complexities where nations like the U.S., China, Russia, and regional blocs such as the EU play crucial roles in defining global stability.

Energy routes, maritime dominance, and technological superiority now define international power. From the South China Sea to the Arctic, geopolitical rivalries are increasingly connected with economic interdependence and ideological conflict. My project examines these evolving realities and how states employ strategic narratives, soft power, and economic leverage to achieve dominance.

The ultimate goal of this study is to bridge academic theories with real-world policy implications — providing insight into how emerging powers are reshaping the world order through diplomacy, trade routes, and cyber influence.
